Chronology of Star Trek

Starfleet has always been about more than just starships and warp drives—it’s about curiosity, courage, and the relentless pursuit of the unknown. From Earth’s first steps into deep space to the founding of the United Federation of Planets, this page dives into the incredible journey that shaped Starfleet into the organization we know today. Discover how legendary captains and crews helped expand the Federation, the technological breakthroughs that made interstellar travel possible, and the defining moments that changed Starfleet forever. Whether you're here to learn about first contact missions, historic battles, or the ideals that guide Starfleet's explorers, there's always something new to uncover.

So grab a cup of replicated coffee, settle in, and start exploring—the stars are calling!

History

  • Star Trek: Enterprise (2151–2161) The earliest series in the timeline, following Captain Jonathan Archer and the first warp-5 starship, the NX-01 Enterprise. A prequel exploring humanity’s first deep-space voyages and the founding of the Federation.
  • Star Trek: Discovery (2256–2258, then 32nd century) Starts in the era just before The Original Series, with Michael Burnham navigating galactic conflicts and Starfleet mysteries. After a dramatic time jump, Discovery explores the 32nd century, where the Federation’s future hangs in the balance.
  • Star Trek: Strange New Worlds (2259–2268) A return to classic Trek-style adventures, following Captain Pike, Spock, and the crew of the USS Enterprise before Captain Kirk takes command.
  • Star Trek: The Original Series (2265–2269) The legendary voyages of Captain Kirk, Spock, and the Enterprise, boldly going where no one has gone before.
  • Star Trek: The Animated Series (2269–2270) An animated continuation of The Original Series, with wild sci-fi concepts too ambitious for live action at the time.
  • Star Trek: The Next Generation (2364–2370) Set nearly a century after Kirk, this series follows Captain Picard’s Enterprise-D, filled with diplomacy, exploration, and existential dilemmas.
  • Star Trek: Deep Space Nine (2369–2375) Taking place alongside later seasons of The Next Generation, DS9 focuses on a space station near a wormhole, tackling war, politics, and moral conflicts.
  • Star Trek: Voyager (2371–2378) Captain Janeway and her crew are stranded 70,000 light-years from Earth, struggling to return home while encountering strange civilizations and dangerous foes in the Delta Quadrant.
  • Star Trek: Lower Decks (2380–Present) A comedic look at the lesser-known crew members of a mid-tier Federation ship. Set shortly after the events of Voyager and Deep Space Nine.
  • Star Trek: Prodigy (2383–Present) An animated series following alien teenagers who discover a derelict Starfleet vessel and learn what it means to be part of the Federation.
  • Star Trek: Picard (2399–2401) A sequel to The Next Generation, focusing on an aging Admiral Picard as he faces new personal and galactic crises.
  • Star Trek: Discovery (32nd Century) After a massive time jump, Discovery explores a far-future Federation, introducing new technologies, civilizations, and challenges.
